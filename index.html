The Proposal

If AI learns from you, it owes you.

Right now, your data flows to platforms and AI models for free. Every assignment you upload, every search you run, every route you drive. It all trains systems worth trillions. But, you don't see a dime. Then you pay a subscription to access the product your data helped build.

The Data Savings Act changes that. It establishes that your data is a financial asset you own, held in a personal Data Savings Account that compounds over time. Think of it like a pension for the future. The digital footprint you leave contributes both to AI's future and your own financial future.

This isn't anti-AI. It's pro-participation. Every AI system on Earth requires continuous streams of human-generated data to function. People aren't a problem to be subsidized. People are the asset AI is built on. The Act ensures everyone who contributes enjoys the upside.

Why not UBI?

The mainstream answer is Universal Basic Income. Here's why we disagree.

The Subsidy Path

UBI

"Tax the rich and send everyone a check." It's a compassionate idea built on a defeatist premise: that human economic contribution is obsolete.

It makes you a recipient. It says your value ended. The government will take care of it from here.

The Ownership Path

Data Savings

Your data has real economic value. The Act turns that value into a compounding asset in an account you control, not a check that someone else decides to send.

It makes you an owner. It says your contribution is ongoing, growing, and yours.
